Why?

The output of Generative AI is—and will continue to be—hard to detect. Because it's so convincing, tools to understand the role Generative AI played in the creation of content are needed.

Authors, artists, and musicians may want a way to explicitly state that a work was entirely created by them. Or, they may wish to be transparent about how much help they got in the creation of their work. There's a difference in having a Generative AI fix typos than having it write prose that you later edit.

If these differences are important to others, the AI Content Declaration is an easy way to clarify them in your own work. It doesn't mean that Generative AI is bad or unethical, but it benefits everyone to know the exact role it plays in the media we consume.
